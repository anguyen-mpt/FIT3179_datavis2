Social Competency Development of Children in Victoria

This webpage aims to provide inisghts into the social competency development of Victorian children. It uses data from the Australian Early Development Census and the Australian Bureau of Statistics.

Children's social competency development across Australia

In Australia, the proportion of children with vulnerable social competency development varies significantly by state. For example, Northern Territory has the largest proportion of children with vulnerable social competency at 21.7%. On the other hand, Western Australia has the lowest proportion at 7.6%. For Victoria, the figure is 9%, which is not very high compared to other states. However, it is still worth examining.

Notes: The unit in this figure is % as it refers to the proportion of children with vulnerable social competence development

Children's vulnerable development in Victoria by domain

The figure on the right shows the vulnerable development of Victorian children by different domain. Social competence remains the area where most children are vulnerable. By contrast, Victorian children tend to perform well in their language development. However, more children have been doing badly in these two domains recently.

Social competence development of Victorian children in 2021

Most children in Victoria are on the right track of their social competence development. However, there is still more than 20% of children at risk or vulnerable in their social competence.

Vulnerable social competency development of Victorian children by LGA

The health development score varies greatly across Victoria. More children in regional LGAs suffer from vulnerable social competence development than children in metropolitan LGAs.

There are about 10 LGAs that have high proportion of children with ulnerable social competence development. They include Swan Hill, Hindmarsh, Aarat, Central Goldfields, among many others. .

Social advantage of living areas and impacts on children's social competency development

Index of Relative Socio-economic Disadvantage (IRSD) is a measures of relative disadvantage of an area in Australia. It is developed by ABS. A low score indicates relatively greater disadvantage in general and vice versa.

The bubble plot shows that there is a negative correlation between social advantage and social competence development of children. LGAs that have high IRSD score tend to have less children suffering from vulnerable social competence development.
