Social Competency Development of Children in Victoria

This webpage aims to provide inisghts into the social competency development of Victorian children through different visualisations. It uses data from the Australian Early Development Census and the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS).

All visualisations come with tooltips for easy navigation of data. To see a tooltip, hover your cursor over an element of the visualisations.

Children's social competence development across Australia

In Australia, the proportion of children with vulnerable social competence development varies significantly by state.

Northern Territory has the largest proportion of children with vulnerable social competency (21.7%). For Victoria, 9% of children are vulnerable in their social competence development. While this is not very high compared to other states, it is not the lowest (7.6% of Western Australia).

Note: The unit in this figure is % as it refers to the proportion of children with vulnerable social competence development

Children's vulnerable development in Victoria by domain

Social competence remains the area where most Victorian children have been vulnerable in development for the last decade. By contrast, they tend to perform well in their language development. However, more children have been doing badly in these two domains recently.

Social competence development of Victorian children in 2021

Most children in Victoria are on the right track of their social competence development. However, there is still more than 20% of children at risk or vulnerable in their social competence.

This raises the need for communities to invest to improve the outcome, especially in some disadvantaged areas.

Vulnerable social competency development of Victorian children by LGA

The social competence development score varies greatly across Victoria. More children in regional LGAs suffer from vulnerable social competence development than children in metropolitan LGAs.

There are about 10 LGAs that have high proportion of children with vulnerable social competence development. They include Swan Hill, Hindmarsh, Aarat, Central Goldfields, among many others.

Social disadvantage of living areas and impacts on children's social competency development

Index of Relative Socio-economic Disadvantage (IRSD) is a measures of relative disadvantage of an area in Australia. A low score indicates relatively greater disadvantage in general and vice versa. More information about this can be found on the ABS website.

There might be a relationship between the social disadvantage of an LGA and children's social competence development in that area. The following figures confirm this hypothesis.

The bubble plot shows that there is a negative correlation between social advantage and social competence development of children. LGAs with high IRSD score tend to have less children suffering from vulnerable social competence development.

The bar chart shows top 10 LGAs with the lowest IRSD scores, meaning they are the greatest disadvantage areas. Some of these LGAs do have high proportion of children with vulnerable social competence development, as illustrated in the map.
